The Case For Each Option

Option A — Keep Freelancing Only

Case for it: Familiar, low-risk, no new systems to learn — just keep submitting proposals.
Hidden upside: Consistency builds a visible track record clients eventually notice.
Biggest weakness: Same channel, same silence — you're repeating a pattern with no new signal.
Best if you value: Independence and flexible hours.

Option B — Diversify: Freelance + Internship + Job Applications

Case for it: More channels means more shots at your first paid "yes" — internships and jobs often respond faster than cold freelance bids.
Hidden upside: A single internship or job offer gives you a credibility anchor that makes future freelance pitches stronger.
Biggest weakness: Takes more effort to manage multiple application types at once.
Best if you value: Speed to your first paid opportunity, above all else.

Option C — Pause & Upskill More Before Applying

Case for it: Feels safe — more preparation, less exposure to rejection.
Hidden upside: Could sharpen a genuinely weak spot, if one exists.
Biggest weakness: This is the exact 2023 pattern you already named as a mistake.
Best if you value: Comfort over momentum.
