# Red Flags

> All flags listed in descending order of severity.

---

## ❌ Severity 9/10 — Junior Title, Senior Expectations

**What it says:** "Junior Full Stack Developer with 5+ years of experience"

**Why it's a red flag:**
"Junior" is an entry-level designation, typically 0–2 years of experience. Pairing it with a 5+ year requirement is contradictory and suggests the company wants to pay junior salaries for senior-level output. Responsibilities like designing architecture, leading client meetings, and mentoring others reinforce this.

---

## ❌ Severity 9/10 — Impossible Tech Stack

**What it says:** React, Angular, Vue + Node.js, Python, Java, Golang + AWS, Azure, GCP + Docker, Kubernetes + AI/ML

**Why it's a red flag:**
Demanding simultaneous mastery of 3 frontend frameworks, 4 backend languages, 3 cloud platforms, and containerization tools is unrealistic for any single developer — let alone a junior one. This usually indicates the company doesn't know what they need, or is trying to hire one person to do the work of an entire team.

---

## ❌ Severity 8/10 — Fake Remote with Hidden Relocation

**What it says:** "Remote (Relocation to Bengaluru may be required after probation)"

**Why it's a red flag:**
Marketing a role as Remote while hiding a mandatory relocation clause is deceptive. Candidates who accept based on the remote offer may find themselves pressured to move — or lose the job for refusing. This should have been listed as "Hybrid / In-office after probation."

---

## ⚠️ Severity 8/10 — 24/7 Support Requirement

**What it says:** "Provide 24/7 support when needed"

**Why it's a red flag:**
For an 8-month-old startup with 12 people, there is unlikely to be a formal on-call rotation or incident response system. "When needed" is vague and can be stretched to mean constant availability. This is a documented path to burnout.

---

## ⚠️ Severity 7/10 — Hustle Culture Language + No Salary

**What it says:** "Work hard, play hard," "fast-paced," "thrive under pressure," "we are like a family" — with no salary range disclosed.

**Why it's a red flag:**
Each of these phrases is a well-known signal of a toxic workplace. "Like a family" in particular is used to blur professional boundaries. The absence of a salary range means the company retains full negotiating power — almost always to the candidate's disadvantage.

---

## ⚠️ Severity 7/10 — Unstable Early-Stage Company

**What it says:** Founded 8 months ago, 12 employees, no funding disclosed, rapid hiring, long-hours reviews.

**Why it's a red flag:**
The combination of extreme youth, no financial transparency, and negative employee sentiment creates a high-risk employment profile. Rapid hiring at this stage can mean high attrition — people are leaving as fast as they're being replaced.
