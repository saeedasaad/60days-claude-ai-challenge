# Smart Interview Questions

> These 5 questions are designed to validate the red flags identified in the risk analysis. Ask them early — ideally in the first screening call. Pay close attention not just to the answers, but to how the interviewer *reacts* to being asked.

---

## Question 1 — Validate the Tech Stack Contradiction

> *"The job description lists React, Angular, and Vue together. Which framework does the team actually use day-to-day, and why are the others included in the listing?"*

**What you're testing:** Whether the JD was written carelessly or deliberately inflated.
**Green answer:** A specific framework with a clear reason.
**Red answer:** Vague responses like "we use all of them" or visible discomfort with the question.

---

## Question 2 — Confirm the Remote Policy in Writing

> *"The listing says Remote, but also mentions possible relocation to Bengaluru after probation. Can you confirm the long-term work location policy — and provide that in writing before I make a decision?"*

**What you're testing:** Whether the remote claim is genuine or a recruitment tactic.
**Green answer:** Clear, documented policy with specifics on timeline and conditions.
**Red answer:** Hesitation, vague "we'll figure it out later," or resistance to putting it in writing.

---

## Question 3 — Understand the 24/7 Support Expectation

> *"The JD mentions providing 24/7 support when needed. How many incidents requiring off-hours response have occurred in the past 3 months? Is there a structured on-call rotation with compensation?"*

**What you're testing:** Whether "24/7 support" is a theoretical edge case or a regular expectation.
**Green answer:** Specific numbers, a defined rotation, and mention of compensatory time or pay.
**Red answer:** "It rarely happens" with no system in place, or normalization of always being available.

---

## Question 4 — Probe Financial Stability

> *"The company is about 8 months old — has the team closed any funding round yet, and what does the current runway look like?"*

**What you're testing:** Whether the company can sustain payroll for the next 12–18 months.
**Green answer:** Specific funding information or honest acknowledgment of bootstrapped status with revenue data.
**Red answer:** Deflection, "we can't share that," or obvious discomfort — these are signals of financial fragility.

---

## Question 5 — Challenge the Work-Life Balance Claims

> *"Employee reviews mention long working hours. What's the realistic average workweek for this role, and how does leadership respond when someone raises concerns about overwork?"*

**What you're testing:** Whether the culture is genuinely supportive or whether overwork is normalized and unaddressed.
**Green answer:** Honest acknowledgment of startup intensity with concrete examples of how boundaries are respected.
**Red answer:** Dismissal of the reviews, "we're a family so it doesn't feel like work," or inability to give a straight answer on hours.

---

## Bonus Tip

> If the interviewer seems **perturbed** — unsettled or irritated — by any of these questions, that reaction itself is data. Healthy workplaces welcome candidates who ask thoughtful, direct questions. Companies with something to hide treat scrutiny as a threat.

**Word note — Perturbed:** means visibly disturbed or made uneasy. A good interviewer should never feel threatened by professional questions.
